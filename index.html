Aerial Drones

Missions With 5G And Starlink Support

Context

In a rapidly evolving technological world, our reliance on effective communication networks is more critical than ever. When disasters strike—whether natural, like wildfires, hurricanes, and earthquakes, or man-made—the damage to infrastructure can have far-reaching consequences. Among the hardest hit are often our telecommunication networks, which serve as lifelines, connecting victims to rescuers, families to loved ones, and communities to vital updates and services. When these critical networks go offline, the consequences can be dire, delaying rescue efforts, creating chaos, and exacerbating the challenges faced by affected communities.

Understanding the immense importance of sustaining communications during these critical times, we embarked on a mission to provide a robust, adaptable, and rapid solution. Drones, with their flexibility, maneuverability, and speed, presented an enticing answer. Equipped with the necessary tools, they can effectively serve as airborne cell towers, bringing back online the crucial 5G communication networks in disaster-hit areas.

Our project aims to harness the potential of drone technology to bridge the communication gap during these challenging times. By deploying drones that can establish 5G connectivity, we hope to provide immediate relief in terms of communication infrastructure. This not only aids in effective disaster response but also ensures that people in affected areas are not cut off from the world, helping them reach out for assistance and connect with loved ones.

Goals


Primary

Make a drone network that can share 5G or, in case of a missing connection, share a Starlink connection using 5G


Secondary

Make the mission planner platform more dynamic

Make a Live Feed possible, to use in the tourism area
